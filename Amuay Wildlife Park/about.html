About

Enriching the lives of animals by bringing out their wild side.

Amuay Wildlife Park is a nature reserve situated 350 km west of the Venezuelan capital. The park's wide range of animal and plant life extends over 72 km2 through varying grassland, hills, and marshes. The Venezuelan government prohibits vehicular traffic through the area, other than by scheduled tours along specified dirt tracks, to protect the landscape, to protect visitors, and to preserve flora and fauna.



The Amuay Wildlife Park covers 630 hectares, 594 of which are dedicated to the conservation of flora and fauna, as well as the protection of the local hydrological basin. The other 36 hectares are developed and these areas consist of seven specific environments in which different species of animals are distributed in environments that seek to simulate their natural habitat.



Monkey Forest: In this habitat there are different species of monkeys, most of them South American in particular, and all outdoors.



Avifauna Lagoon: a beautiful environment where you can see herons, flamingos, black swans, road ducks, Galapagos, red-crowned and water dolphins, all species inhabiting the large open lagoon.



The South American plains: here you can see creatures from the continent and the country, such as: wild boars, American camels, horses, iguanas, foxes and even tapirs, among other species.



African plains: an area where you can observe African animals such as: African elephants, hippos, ostriches, deer and buffalo. There are also the ruins of the colonial Santa Cruz hacienda.



The Rim Zone: where alligators of different origins coexist, such as the Orinoco alligator, the Mississippi alligator, baboons, turtles and rat monkeys, as well as parrots, macaws and parakeets.



Feline areas: areas of the park where African lions, pumas, cougars, jaguars and Bengal tigers are found.



Contact area: a specific area of the park where people can freely approach and share the large number of sheep, goats and ducks, or feed them with special food for sale in the same place.



In addition to the natural diversity on offer, at Amuay Wildlife Park there are environmental guides, environmental education programmes, animal research and breeding of endangered species, forums, lectures and seminars.
